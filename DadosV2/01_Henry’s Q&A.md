**1. “Who are the global participants?”**  
  
**Reviewer concern**  
  
The expression “global participants” may create ambiguity. Does it include retail, banks, institutions, or others?  
  
**Answer**  
  
“Global participants” refers broadly to any permitted user of the protocol who interacts with its utility structure. This includes:  
	•	Individuals who satisfy jurisdictional and KYC requirements  
	•	Entities such as energy partners or technical integrators  
	•	Institutional observers who use the transparency layer to monitor operations  
  
It does not imply that DGTEnergy is targeting regulated financial allocators in the sense of a fund or structured product. It simply indicates that, subject to local compliance rules, access is not geographically limited by design.  
  
**Where this was integrated in the Whitepaper**  
	•	Section **1.1 Mission and Context** clarifies the role of participants as users of a utility token protocol and not as holders of financial instruments.  
	•	Section **0. Key Takeaways** frames participation as functional and coordination focused rather than financial.

**5. “Why does this process not convert DGT3 into a financial instrument? There is an operational value reference behind the token.”**  
  
**Reviewer concern**  
  
A strong, technically clear explanation was requested to show why DGT3 remains a utility token, even though the protocol refers to operational cycles and verified performance.  
  
**Answer**  
  
The Whitepaper now explains, in multiple sections, that DGT3 does not become a financial instrument because:  
	•	It represents access to a coordination layer, not claims over assets  
	•	It does not grant rights to receivables, energy bills or cash flows  
	•	It does not grant ownership, control, or beneficial interest in Billi or any partner  
	•	It does not confer redemption rights or settlement against the Treasury  
	•	It does not pool capital into a managed exposure with financial allocation decisions  
	•	It does not distribute financial yields or performance rights  
  
Operational results from partners are treated as verified inputs that feed the coordination engine, not as something that DGT3 holders have a legal right to receive. Functional outputs in staking are always conditional, protocol driven and described as utilities, not entitlements.

**6. “How does the complete cycle work? How does a participant exit? Who provides liquidity? Does the Treasury interact with the P2P Desk?”**  
  
**Reviewer concern**  
  
The reviewer asked for explicit clarity about:  
	•	How participants enter and exit  
	•	How circulation and liquidity are formed  
	•	Whether DGTEnergy depends on third party exchanges  
	•	Whether the Treasury participates in the P2P Desk and, if so, how  
  
**Answer**  
  
The final version clarifies the liquidity and circulation model in detail, including the Treasury’s limited role.  
	1.	**Entry into the protocol**  
Participants acquire DGT3 directly from the DGTEnergy DApp during the whitelist and deterministic sale rounds. USDT flows into the Treasury and is used for operational deployment through De Goat Capital AG, in line with the Tokenomics.  
	2.	**Secondary transfers and exit**  
Participants who wish to buy or sell DGT3 after the initial sale interact through the P2P Desk, which is a KYC controlled, escrow based environment. Transfers are participant to participant under protocol level rules.  
	3.	**No reliance on external exchanges**  
The protocol does not depend on centralized or decentralized exchanges for circulation. The P2P Desk is the primary designed venue for secondary activity, incorporating:  
  
	•	KYC and compliance checks  
	•	Price floor rules based on protocol parameters  
	•	Escrow mechanisms for USDT and DGT3  
	•	Event logging for transparency and audit  
  
	4.	**Treasury as a conditional buyer on the Desk**  
The Whitepaper now specifies that the Treasury may act as a buyer of DGT3 on the P2P Desk under predefined and limited conditions:  
  
	•	All purchases are executed under strict monthly caps, defined as a percentage of circulating supply  
	•	Treasury activity respects Desk pricing rules and does not override the price floor for general participants  
	•	Interaction is always framed as an operational tool to support coordination cycles and reinvestment rules, not as a market making or price management mechanism  
  
	5.	**Reinvestment and Desk interaction**  
The reinvestment framework allows part of the verified operational performance, once reconverted into USDT, to be:  
  
	•	Redeployed into new operational cycles  
	•	Used, under strict limits, to buy DGT3 on the P2P Desk as part of reinvestment logic  
  
This is positioned as a coordination function, not as a guarantee of exit or as a buyback program.  
	6.	**No liquidity guarantees**  
The document is explicit that:  
  
	•	Liquidity is not promised or guaranteed  
	•	The Treasury’s participation on the Desk does not create any assurance of being able to sell at a given price or within a given timeframe  
	•	All such actions remain conditional, operationally justified and fully transparent  
  
**Where this was integrated**  
	•	Section **3.2 End to End Operational Flow**: adds explicit mention of Treasury conditional buying under caps and compliance.  
	•	Section **3.3 Access Through Utility**: clarifies that secondary transfers occur via the P2P Desk and that Treasury participation is limited, capped and non guarantee forming.  
	•	Section **5.6 Treasury Architecture**: describes Treasury as a conditional buyer within predefined rules and monthly caps.  
	•	Section **5.7 Reinvestment Framework**: explains that part of reinvestable USDT may be used to buy DGT3 on the Desk under strict limits and controls.  
	•	Section **9.2 Phases**: in the Reinvestment Engine phase, explicitly refers to the possibility of routing part of reinvestable USDT to buy DGT3 on the P2P Desk under predefined limits.

**9. “Clarify how staking can output USDT without implying a financial product.”**  
  
**Reviewer concern**  
  
The reviewer requested a compliant explanation for:  
	•	Why the possibility of receiving USDT in staking does not create financial rights  
	•	How to frame this design as a utility mechanism, not as a structured financial product  
	•	How to keep the protocol aligned with MiCA and similar guidelines  
  
**Answer**  
  
The Whitepaper clarifies the following points:  
	•	Staking is framed as a participation activation inside the coordination layer, not as a financial allocation  
	•	Functional outputs in USDT are entirely conditional on verified operational cycles and protocol level decisions  
	•	Participants have no rights over receivables, partners or any operational entity  
	•	There is no guarantee of frequency, amount or continuity of outputs  
	•	Outputs are described as functional and coordination related, not as promised distributions  
  
The text explicitly avoids economic language associated with financial instruments and maintains a focus on operational coordination.
